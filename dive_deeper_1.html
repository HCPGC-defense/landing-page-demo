This continuous threat has now reached its pivotal moment. We need your immediate help as a concerned citizen to activate now with us to help save this 100 year old Historic Landmark in the heart of Baton Rouge.

We need you to demand that the golf course retain its current footprint, scale and scope. Tell the officials who represent you in city parish government (i.e. your parish council representative), tell the newspaper (call their switchboard and complain), tell the television broadcasters (call their switchboard and complain), tell self annoited and self appointed at Baton Rouge Area Foundation (BRAF), tell your neighbors that we do not want to re-imagine City Park, we do not want to change City Park if that entails destroying the existing golf course, reducing its historic nine-hole format, shrinking the size of the fairways and/or destroying or minimizing this cultural icon of the City of Baton Rouge that has severed as a recreational resource for all of East Baton Rouge Parish and State of Louisiana since its creation in 1926 over a century ago.

Progress does not mean bulldozers, wine shacks beneath a 120 decibel interstate, boathouses, greek outdoor ampitheaters and running trails to replace the timeless beauty of City Park Golf Course's historic fairways and greens. We say boo hoo to developers arboretuem too (you already have one next to the Main Library on Goodwood).

City Park Golf Course was designed to play golf and provide a green environment with an active lifestyle. It supports scenic beauty for walkers, joggers, golfers, and the general public just as it is. Yes, perhaps it could be a nicer golf course, but losing any piece of it will be a disaster.

Are you the citizens of East Baton Rouge Parish aware that BREC has a 14.9 mil taxation slush fund, while the East Baton Rouge Parish general fund is supported by a mere 2.96 mil tax ? It's time we put an end to the land theft at City Park Golf Course and tell every mayor in the Parish to focus on roads, sewer, drainage, and crime."

Admittedly, in our heart, we want to support BREC, we want to support BRAF, but when special interest take over these organizations, rather than improving the existing public golf course, we find ourselves desperately ringing the alarm to save the very existence of the golf course before it is bulldozed forever by private interest hiding under the banner of public progressive good.

When BREC can publicly announce an memorialize in an offical public meeting that the footprint, scale and scope of the Historic City Park Golf Course will remain for unmolested for generations to come, we will forgive them for daring to destroy City Park Golf Course Baton Rouge, a Historic Landmark that we must preserve entirely for the future. Only then can we support future milage for BREC. If the money is to be misused and misappropriated, then we find it best untaxed.

We can promise every BREC official, if you steal the Historic City Park Golf Course, if you shrink its size, if you alter its use; we will come for you at the next milage tax vote; we will gut your public funds and send you packing.
